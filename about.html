About Frontline Healthcare Digital

Frontline Healthcare Digital was built from the inside out, not by a marketing agency that learned about healthcare, but by someone who lived it for over 30 years.

It started in 1990 as a volunteer firefighter/EMT and By 1996, EMS was a career. By 2000, a full-time paramedic role with one of Pennsylvania's three largest ambulance services, where the job didn't stop at patient care. Maintaining the organization's computer network, managing its website, and eventually stepping into the role of Information Systems Officer meant owning the full picture: CAD software, third-party billing systems, EDI workflows, documentation cycles, and the operational dependencies that tie all of it together.

That combination of field experience and systems knowledge is rare. Most people who understand ambulance billing have never run a call. Most people who build healthcare websites have never touched an ePCR.

After retiring from the field in 2016, that career path continued into emsChart, a web-based patient care reporting platform built specifically for EMS. Then consulting work across financial and utility sectors. Then back to the billing world as a Product Support Analyst for the same platform used in the field for years.

Frontline Healthcare Digital exists because most EMS agencies and healthcare organizations are being underserved digitally, not for lack of options, but because the people building their websites and workflows don't understand what actually happens on a shift, in a billing department, or inside a CAD system.

We do.
